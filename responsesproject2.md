1 : https://yejiweji.github.io/CART263_Project2_-FINAL-/
The interface presents itself as an entirely ordinary museum ticketing form clean layout, price listed, number of tickets, a buy button. There is a single question sitting in the middle;: Are you a person of colour? Which is rather humorous (in a way) since it increases the price depending on a yes or no.
There are certain juxtapositions when scrolling through a gallery of framed images of PSY, Squid Game, Jackie Chan, K-pop figures(etc), then traditional Korean and Chinese garments on mannequins. Everything is presented with complete gallery sincerity as per string lights overhead, ornate frames, openable doors. The curatorial language is rather on point with the fact that certain elements (again unsure if this is intentional) blur depending on its value? When arrived at the gift shop, I am unsure if the loop should close here but the commentary from MR FORTUNE is another fun element.

2: https://yatsukki.github.io/cart263/Project1/draw.html

This is a drawing application dressed as a Windows 98 desktop program complete with a fake title bar reading Untitled.psd, (I am unsure if this was intentional) the very stylized scrollbar UI elements along the edges, and adorable cats and glamorous decorations. It reads as nostalgia, the skeuomorphic UI design of early 2000s software reproduced in a browser, which is inherently adapted because that design language was trying to look like something physical but never quite.
The most interesting technical element is the window chrome, building UI components that look like operating system decorations out of positioned images and HTML elements. This requires careful attention to the specific visual grammar of that period such as the exact “weight” of a scrollbar, or the correct shape of a close button, and the smile icon tucked in the corner.

3: https://lannacheck-ops.github.io/cart263/project-1/

The title expresses to you exactly what this is about and also exactly what you are about to become. Skinner's Box is the famous operant conditioning experiment of an animal in a controlled environment stuck in a reward based loop and anguish. The piece frames the user within as explicitly the subject. There is a User Incentive label, a Notify button, a character whose Heart Variability can apparently be adjusted (but I am having trouble with that). The language is clinical and the implication is not subtle regarding the feelings of entrapment. HRV is a real biometric used to measure stress, autonomic nervous system activity and emotional regulation. Using it rather than something more cartoonishly gamified like points or a health bar makes the piece feel genuinely close to how it refuses engagement optimisation.
